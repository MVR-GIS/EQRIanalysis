    • 1: Is there an Engineering Technical Lead (TL) assigned to the project with an active professional registration (e.g., P.E., R.A., P.L.A., P.G., etc.) or waiver from the Chief of the District’s Engineering Function?
    • 2: Does the design team have all the necessary members (i.e., technical disciplines) assigned given the project scope?
    • 3: Do the QA/QC review teams have all the necessary members (i.e., technical disciplines) assigned given the project scope?
    • 4: Does the design team have sufficient relevant design experience with projects of similar scope and complexity?
    • 5: Are senior staff mentors assigned to all design team members with less than two years of professional design experience?
    • 6: Do the design and review team members have capacity to actively support the project?
    • 7: Have the relevant Mandatory Centers of Expertise (MCX), Technical Centers of Expertise (TCX), and/or Centers of Standardization (COS) been identified and engaged on the project?
    • 8: Have the relevant permitting authorities been identified and engaged on the project?
    • 9: Are the assumptions made during planning/programming still valid (e.g., real estate, environmental, geotechnical, hydraulics and hydrology, survey, and other pertinent assumptions)?
    • 10: At the start of design, is there a sufficient level of scope definition and design maturity to support a Class 3 estimate?
    • 11: Was the Total Project Cost Summary (TPCS) certified within the last two years?
    • 12: At the current time, can the project be implemented without deviations from mandatory design criteria or receiving an approved waiver from HQUSACE?
    • 13: Have lessons learned been evaluated and incorporated into the design?
    • 14: Did the Project Delivery Team (PDT) establish design expectations by milestone and communicate those in the Project Management Plan (PMP), Architect-Engineer (A-E) contract, and/or brokered work agreement, etc.?
    • 15: Does the project schedule include all standard practice design milestones?
    • 16: Does the project schedule include sufficient design time to accomplish all design related milestones?
    • 17: Does the project schedule include sufficient time for Quality Control (QC) and Quality Assurance (QA) reviews during all required design submittals?
    • 18: Does the project schedule include sufficient time to complete engineering investigations and surveys (e.g., cultural, geotechnical, environmental, hazardous materials, etc.) in time to adequately inform the design?
    • 19: Does the project schedule include sufficient time to complete permitting process(es)?
    • 20: Is the current design team schedule in alignment with the project schedule in the Project Management Plan (PMP)?
    • 21: Does the project have a current, approved project-specific Quality Management Plan (QMP)/Review Plan (RP)?
    • 22: Did District Quality Control (DQC) occur prior, and not concurrent with, to the Independent Technical Review (ITR)/Agency Technical Review (ATR)/Biddability, Constructability, Operability, Environmental and Sustainability (BCOES) Review?
    • 23: Was the Quality Management Plan (QMP)/Review Plan (RP) followed for this review milestone?
    • 24: Have all computations gone through a rigorous Quality Control (QC) process (e.g., a “red dot” review)? (Note: For A-E work, it is incumbent upon the Technical Lead (TL) to ensure the A-E has performed this level of review.)
    • 25: Have all critical graphics/plans gone through a rigorous QC process (e.g., a “red dot” review)? (Note: For A-E work, it is incumbent upon the Technical Lead (TL) to ensure the A-E has performed this level of review.)
    • 26: Have all specifications gone through a rigorous QC process? (Note: For A-E work, it is incumbent upon the Technical Lead (TL) to ensure the A-E has performed this level of review.)
    • 27: Have all models gone through a rigorous QC process? (e.g., a “red dot” review, clash detection, etc.)? (Note: For A-E work, it is incumbent upon the Technical Lead (TL) to ensure the A-E has performed this level of review.)
    • 28: Has a Biddability, Constructability, Operability, Environmental, and Sustainability (BCOES) review been performed in compliance with the ER?
    • 29: Have District Quality Control (DQC), Independent Technical Review (ITR)/Agency Technical Review (ATR), and Biddability, Constructability, Operability, Environmental, and Sustainability (BCOES) certifications been completed?
    • 30: Has the Quality Management Plan (QMP)/Review Plan (RP) been updated to respond to significant project changes?
    • 31: Does the design adequately accomplish the project scope, intent, and functional and technical objectives as defined in the current Project Management Plan (PMP)?
    • 32: Did the deliverable meet established design milestone expectations?
    • 33: Is the design maturity sufficient to generate the required Class of estimate for this milestone? (Class 3 at 35%, Class 2 at 65%, and Class 1 at 95%/100%)
    • 34: Does the project have a current risk register?
    • 35: Was the project budget verified with a Cost Schedule Risk Analysis (CSRA) at concept design (i.e., 35%)?
    • 36: Did the Geographic District validate and/or develop the Programmed Amount (PA)?
    • 37: Has there been turnover on your design or review team that may affect the team’s ability to develop a quality product?
    • 38: What is your level of confidence in the quality of the engineering design efforts on this project?
